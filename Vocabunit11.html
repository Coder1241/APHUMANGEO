Unit 11 Vocab
Organic Agriculture Farming that avoids the use of chemicals.
Agriculture The tending to crops and animals to produce food and fibers.
Primary Economic Activity Economic activity relating to the direct extraction of resources.
Secondary Economic Activity Economic activity relating to taking those resources and turning them into goods/products.
Tertiary Economic Activity Economic activity relating to services.
Quaternary Economic Activity Services relating to the collection and processing of information.
Quinary Economic Activity Service industries that require a high level of skill.
Plant Domestication The combination of human settlements, forest margins, and fresh water may have lead to the start of Root Crops. Essentially, growing plants to harvest them.
Root Crops Crops that are reproduced by Cultivating the roots or cuttings from the plants.
Seed Crops Plants that are reproduced by Cultivating the seeds.
First Agricultural Revolution This Revolution was marked by Seed Crops, this revolution focused more on the location of Agricultural hearths and what kinds of Agricultural innovations took place in that hearth.
Animal Domestication The use of animals to sustain products like food or goods to a population.
Subsistence Agriculture Subsistence farming animals or crops. Or hard labor farming. Require a LARGE workforce.
Shifting Cultivation Cultivation of crops in a tropical area or forest, and most the forest vegetation has been removed due to this farming, and the slash and burn method, thus, these clearings are abandoned after a few years.
Slash-and-Burn agriculture Essentially the same as Shifting Cultivation, it’s large clearing of land and then abandoning it after the land has been used.
Second Agricultural Revolution Benefitting from Industrialization, this period witnessed improved cultivation, harvesting and storage of farm produce.
von Thuden Model A model showing where stuff can be farmed around the CBD for highest profits and based on the crop.
Third Agricultural Revolution Currently in progress, however mostly relates to the development of GMO’s.
Green Revolution The use of Bio-technology in order to create disease resistant, fast growing seeds.
Genetically Modified Organisms Organisms that are modified to have specific traits.
