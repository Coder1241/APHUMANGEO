Unit 6 Vocab
Standard language/Official language A language that is purposely published, widely spread and purposely taught
Dialects Standards of languages along regional or ethnic lines.
Isogloss A geographic boundary within a particular linguistic feature occurs.
Mutual intelligibility When two people are able to understand each other when speaking
Language families When viewed at a global scale, each family has multiple languages that are similar, but distant origins.
Backward reconstruction Tracking shifting consonants and cognates back in an effort to reconstruct elements of a common language.
Extinct language A language that cannot be reconstructed, this typically happens when a country switches languages or most of it’s people pass due to conflict.
Nostratic A language believed to be the Ancestor of Proto-Indo-Europeans and many others.
Language divergence When a new language forms due to the dialects due to lack of Spatial interactions among speakers.
Language convergence The cause of two languages to collapse into one.
Renfrew hypothesis A theory that in the first agricultural hearth or the Fertile Cresent gave rise to the three language families, Europe’s Indo-European Languages, North African, and Arabian languages.
Conquest theory A theory that state that the Indo-European language began in Anatolia and dispersed Eastward into South Asia and Westward into Europe.
Romance languages Languages that are in the area that were once controlled by the Roman Empire however it wasn’t overwhelmed.
Germanic languages Languages that reflect the expansion of people out of Northern Europe to the West and to the South
Slavic languages A language developed as Slavic people migrated from a base in present day close to Ukraine 2000 years ago.
Lingua Franca A language used among speakers among different languages for trading purposes.
Pidgin language When two different languages are combined to simplify structure and vocabulary.
Creole language A pidgin language that has developed and has more complex vocabulary and has became the language to the native group of people.
Multilingual states A country that speaks in more than one language.
Toponym The reasoning behind the name.
