Unit 8 Vocab
Political Geography A subdivision of human geography based on the nature and implications of the evolving spatial organization of political governance and political practice on Earth’s Surface.
State/Country States are the same as countries, countries need four things to be a country. 1. Government 2. Population 3. Territory and 4. Sovereign
Territoriality The process by which how some units come to being. The attempt by someone to take control over a certain area.
Sovereignty The ability to make decisions without restrictions
Territorial Integrity The right of a state to defend Sovereign territory against another state.
Peace of Westphalia Peace negotiated to end the Thirty Years war in 1648. Most destructive internal struggle over religion. Caused States to form.
Mercantilism Essentrially dealing with commercialism and trade. An economy based on exporting more than importing in order to make more profit.
Nation A group of people with shared Ancestry, History, and Culture.
Nation state A homogeneous country consisting of one nationality.
Multinational state A heterogenous country made up of many different nationalities.
Multistate nation The act of a Nation stretches across borders and other states.
Stateless nation A group of people who desire their own state. Living inside the borders of another state.
Colonialism Rule by power over people or land, mostly obtained or established because of political structures.
Capitalism A Free market economy where people make personal profit, where the government doesn’t control the economy.
Immanuel Wallerstein A man who developed the world systems theory that put states into three categories, Core, Semi-Periphery, and Periphery.
Core/1st world/MDC A country that is developed and has a lot of wealth/power within the economy. Most core countries are on stage four in Raven steins population pyramid model.
Periphery/3rd world/LDC Periphery meaning the country is most likely not developed and supplies resources to the Semi-Periphery countries to make money. Most of these countries are in Stage two of Raven Steins population pyramid model.
Semi-Periphery/2nd world States that are still developing to become core countries, these states take the resources from Periphery countries and turn them into goods to sell for profit. Core Countries buy from most of these countries. The country is most likely to be in stage 2 or 3 in Raven Steins Population Pyramid Model.
Centripetal Force A force that causes people to come together.
Centrifugal Force A force that divides or separates people.
Devolution The movement of power down from the central government to regional governments.
Federal A state that has a central government but sends equal power to it’s regional governments.
Unitary All power is within the central government.
Territorial Representation A candidate elected from a territorial defined district.
Reapportionment Occurs after the Census, Its when states can gain a membership, lose a membership, or stay the same.
Splitting When Major and Minority populations are spread evenly across the district to ensure a minority vote.
Gerrymandering Boundary Boundaries created upon making as many Gerrymandering districts possible, allowing their party to be the majority vote.
Geometric Boundary A boundary defined as a straight line or an arc.
Physical/political boundary A boundary that is a physical feature on Earths Surface.
Heartland Theory A theory by Halford Mackinder that any political power based in the heart of Eurasia could gain sufficient power to dominate the world.
Geopolitics Ideas that are around the statecraft construct ideas about the geographical circumstances which reinforces their political behaviors and policy choices.
Supranational organizations An Organization composed of three or more states, that forms an administrative structure and in the pursuit of shared goals.
Choke Point A point where the waterways are very narrow, where international trade occurs. If this point was shut down at any time, it could interrupt global trade.
Berlin Conference 1884 A meeting among the European leaders, drawing boundaries to divide Africa.
